Feature selection

In addition to identifying the label we‚Äôll collect for each of our users‚Äô sessions, it‚Äôs worth thinking about the features that may help predict that label. Here are some candidates:

  • Actual wind conditions measured at various locations near the session spot during the session time (more on this later)
  • Wind conditions predicted by weather forecasts for the session spot during the session time
    • Average wind speed in the N-S and E-W directions
    • Gust speed in the N-S and E-W directions
  • User profile
    • Weight
    • Skill level
  • Equipment profile (with different dimensions per sport). For example:
    • Surface area of wing
    • Surface area of hydrofoils
    • Combined weight of board, mast, foils, and fuselage
    • Volume of board

MVP user stories

Keeping in mind the labels and features we‚Äôre interested in collecting, let‚Äôs sketch out some user stories for the Wind Journal MVP.

Logging a session

As a wing foiler or windsurfer, I want to be able to record the time and location of a session along with the equipment I used during that session, and whether I was underpowered, overpowered, or well-powered during the session. I want the forecast and actual wind conditions for my session to be fetched automatically based on the time and location I record.

Reviewing past sessions

As someone who has used Wind Journal to log wind sports sessions, I want to be able to see a compact list of my past sessions. I want to be able to select a particular session and see all of the data associated with that session.

Editing or deleting a session

As a Wind Journal user with an existing logged session, I want to be able to change the data I have entered for that session. I also want to be able to delete sessions I have created.
